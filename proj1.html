Mission 1: Oceanic Plankton Monitoring

Understanding Marine Ecosystems

The Oceanic Plankton Monitoring mission aims to study the distribution and abundance of plankton across various oceanic regions. By analyzing plankton populations, we can gain valuable insights into marine food webs, carbon cycling, and the overall health of ocean ecosystems.

Utilizing advanced sensors and imaging technologies, this mission provides precise monitoring capabilities, offering critical data for scientists and policymakers to make informed decisions regarding marine conservation and climate change mitigation.

Mission Objectives

  • Map global plankton distribution with high spatial resolution.
  • Assess the impact of climate change on marine ecosystems.
  • Support sustainable fisheries management through data-driven insights.
  • Enhance understanding of carbon sequestration processes in oceans.

Timeline

  • 2023: Mission planning and instrument design.
  • 2024: Construction and testing of monitoring equipment.
  • 2025: Launch and initial data collection.
  • 2026 - 2030: Continuous monitoring and data analysis.
