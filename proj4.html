Mission 4: Studying Global Biogeochemical Cycles

Understanding Earthâ€™s Nutrient Flows

This mission focuses on studying global biogeochemical cycles, which are essential for understanding the flow of nutrients and elements in Earth's ecosystems. By monitoring these cycles, we can better understand their impacts on climate, ecology, and human health.

Using advanced satellite sensors, this mission provides critical data on nutrient levels, carbon storage, and ecosystem responses to environmental changes.

Mission Objectives

  • Monitor global nutrient cycles, including carbon, nitrogen, and phosphorus.
  • Assess the impact of human activities on biogeochemical cycles.
  • Enhance understanding of ecosystem responses to climate change.
  • Support sustainable resource management and conservation efforts.

Timeline

  • 2023: Research and development of monitoring techniques.
  • 2024: Pilot studies and initial data collection.
  • 2025: Launch and full deployment of the satellite.
  • 2026 - 2030: Continuous monitoring and data analysis for ecosystem management.
